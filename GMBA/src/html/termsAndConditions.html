The goals and principles of making biodiversity data openly and universally available have been defined in the Memorandum of Understanding on GMBA, paragraph 8 (see the relevant excerpts in the attached Annex).

The Participants who have signed the MoU have expressed their willingness to make biodiversity data available through their nodes to foster scientific research development internationally and to support the public use of these data.

Data providers often participate in several data sharing arrangements at different levels (thematic, community, national, global).

GMBA data sharing should take place within a framework of due attribution.

Therefore, when registering their services with GMBA, the data providers agree as follows:

1. Data Sharing Agreements

  1. Biodiversity data accessible via the GMBA network are openly and universally available to all users within the framework of the GMBA Data Use Agreement and with the terms and conditions that the data provider has identified in its metadata.
  2. GMBA does not assert any intellectual property rights in the data that is made available through its network.
  3. The data provider warrants that they have made the necessary agreements with the original owners of the data that it can make the data available through GMBA network.
  4. The data provider makes reasonable efforts to ensure that the data they serve are accurate.
  5. Responsibility regarding the restriction of access to sensitive data resides with the data provider.
  6. The data provider includes stable and unique identifiers in their data so that the owner of the data is known and for other necessary purposes.
  7. GMBA Secretariat may cache a copy and serve full or partial data further to other users together with the terms and conditions for use set by the data provider. Queries of such data through the GMBA Secretariat are reported to the data provider.
  8. Data providers are endorsed by a GMBA Participant, if applicable, before their metadata is made available by the GMBA Secretariat.
  9. GMBA Secretariat is not liable or responsible, nor are its employees or contractors, for the data contents or their use; or for any loss, damage, claim, cost or expense however it may arise, from an inability to use the GMBA network.

2. Service Levels

GMBA Secretariat
  1. Services provided by the GMBA Secretariat are managed in accordance with the GMBA Work Programme.
  2. GMBA Secretariat's service provision includes software components and updates, interfaces, indexing and registry services, helpdesk, and training to assist the Participants to maintain Internet portals.
GMBA Participants
  1. GMBA Participants keep the GMBA Secretariat informed of their contact and service information.
  2. GMBA Participants maintain services that enable new and existing data providers in their domain to be integrated within GMBA network, and the data owners be identified, as appropriate.

3. Definitions

  • GMBA Participant: Signatory of the GMBA-establishing Memorandum of Understanding (MoU).
  • GMBA Secretariat: Legal entity empowered by the GMBA Participants to enter into contracts, execute the Work Programme, and maintain the central services for the GMBA network.
  • GMBA network: The infrastructure consisting of the central services of the GMBA Secretariat, Participant nodes and data providers. Making data available through GMBA network means registering and advertising the pertinent services via the GMBA central services.
  • Node: A data provider designated by a GMBA Participant that maintains a stable computer gateway that makes data available through the GMBA network.
  • Participant Node: An organisational unit designated by the GMBA Participant to coordinate activities in its domain. It may also provide data.
  • Biodiversity Data: Primary data on specimens, observations, names, taxonomic concepts, and sites, and other data on biological diversity.
  • Metadata: Data describing the attributes and combinations of biodiversity data.
  • Data: Biodiversity data and metadata.
  • Data sharing: The process of and agreements for making data freely and universally available on the Internet.
  • Data provider: A custodian of data making it technically available. This may or may not be the data owner. If not they will have declared to GMBA that they have permission to make the data available.
  • User: Anyone who uses the Internet to access data through the GMBA network.
  • Owner of data: The legal entity possessing the right resulting from the act of creating a digital record. The record may be a product derived from another, possibly non-digital product, which may affect the right.
  • Sensitive data: Any data that the data provider does not want to make available, e.g. precise localities of endangered species.

Annex

GMBA Memorandum of Understanding - Paragraph 8

INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

  1. Applicable Law
    Nothing in this MOU should be read to alter the scope and application of Intellectual Property Rights and benefit sharing agreements as determined under relevant laws, regulations and international agreements of the Participants.
  2. Access to Data
    To the greatest extent possible, GMBA is an open-access facility. All users, whether GMBA Participants or others, should have equal access to data in databases affiliated with or developed by GMBA.
  3. Intellectual Property Rights to Biodiversity Data
    GMBA promotes the free dissemination of biodiversity data and, in particular:
    1. should not assert any proprietary rights to the data in databases that are developed by other organisations and that subsequently become affiliated to GMBA;
    2. should seek, to the greatest extent possible, to make freely and openly available, with the least possible restrictions on reuse, any data commissioned, created or developed directly by GMBA; and
    3. should respect conditions set by data providers that affiliate their databases to GMBA.

    When establishing affiliations or linkages with other databases, GMBA should seek to ensure that the data so made available will not be subject to limitations on the further non-commercial use and dissemination of those data, apart from due attribution of their source.
  4. Attribution
    GMBA should seek to ensure that the source of data is acknowledged and should request that such attribution be maintained in any subsequent use of the data.
  5. Access to Specific Data
    Nothing in this MOU should be read to restrict the right of owners of databases affiliated with GMBA to block access to any data.
  6. Validity of Data
    It should be a condition of access to and use of GMBA that users acknowledge that the validity of the data in any databases affiliated with GMBA cannot be assured. GMBA should disclaim responsibility for the accuracy and reliability of the data as well as for the suitability of its application for any particular purpose.
  7. Legitimacy of Data Collection
    Where the collection of new data has entailed access to biodiversity resources, GMBA should ask for reasonable assurances from the data provider that such access was consistent with applicable laws, regulations and any relevant requirements for prior informed consent.
  8. Intellectual Property Rights to Biodiversity Tools
    GMBA may claim appropriate Intellectual Property Rights available within applicable national jurisdictions over any tools, such as search engines or other software products that are developed by GMBA while carrying out the GMBA Work Programme.
  9. Technology Transfer
    The Participants acknowledge that, subject to any relevant Intellectual Property Rights, GMBA should seek to promote the non-exclusive transfer, on mutually agreed terms, to research institutions, particularly in developing countries, of such informatics technology as it has available, especially in conjunction with training and capacity development programs.
