Region characteristics

The Arctic marine environment is a vast polar region covering nearly 15 million km2 and spanning both the eastern and western hemispheres. It is eliptical in shape with waters from the Pacific entering the Arctic through the Bering Sea and exiting into the Atlantic through the Norwegian and Greenland Seas. It is also fed by several of the largest rivers in the world, including seven in Russia, and has an extensive shelf system that in places extends several thousand kilometers.

Particularly along the eastern edge is internationally recognized for its spectacular granite cliffs, waterfalls, clear streams, Giant Sequoia groves, and biological diversity.
